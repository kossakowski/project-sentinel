### DISAGREEMENT #1 (CRITICAL): False Positive Phone Call — "Bestialski atak Rosji"

| Field | Haiku | Audit |
|-------|-------|-------|
| is_military_event | 1 | 1 |

| affected_countries | ["PL"] | ["PL"] (partial — wreckage found, not targeted) |
| aggressor | RU | RU (attack was on Ukraine, spillover to PL) |

- **Article:** "Bestialski atak Rosji. Użyli blisko 500 dronów i pocisków, zginęła jedna osoba" (GoogleNews/Polskie Radio 24)
- **What happened:** The "500 drones and missiles" attack was the massive April 3 combined Russian strike **on Ukraine** (579 air weapons). One person died **in Ukraine**. The connection to Poland was jets scrambled and drone wreckage later found in Opole. Haiku read the clickbait headline at face value and generated a summary stating "masowy atak na **Polskę**" — factually wrong.
- **Impact:** **THIS TRIGGERED A PHONE CALL.** An urgency-9 classification means "active military attack or invasion on Poland." Russia did not attack Poland. Correct urgency (5-6) would have sent an SMS — the appropriate response for a spillover incident. The corroboration requirement (2+ sources for phone calls) should have prevented this, but the event record shows `source_count=1` — meaning the phone call fired with only 1 source. **The corroboration_required config is set to 1, not 2. This is a configuration problem.**
- **Suggested fix:** (1) Set `alerts.urgency_levels.critical.corroboration_required` to 2 in config. (2) Add a prompt instruction telling the classifier to distinguish between attacks ON target countries vs. attacks on Ukraine with spillover effects. (3) Consider adding headline-debiasing instructions: "Do not take sensationalist headlines at face value — read the full summary."
